About where its from:

Kinkaku-ji Temple, also known as the Golden Pavilion, is a renowned Zen Buddhist temple in Kyoto, Japan. It was initially built in 1397 as a retirement villa for Shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu. The site was formerly the location of a villa owned by statesman Saionji Kintsune. After Yoshimitsu's death in 1408, his son transformed the villa into a temple, as per his father's wishes. The temple is notable for its top two floors, which are completely covered in gold leaf, and it is officially named Rokuon-ji (Deer Garden Temple). Kinkaku-ji is celebrated for its tranquil and beautiful setting, contributing to its status as one of Kyoto's most popular and visually striking landmarks.
